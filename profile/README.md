# DRAINMOD — NC State University

[**⬇ Download Latest Installer →**](https://github.com/drainmod/drainmod-releases/releases/latest)

**DRAINMOD** is a process-based, field-scale hydrological simulation model developed 
in the Department of Biological and Agricultural Engineering at North Carolina State 
University. Originally published by Skaggs (1978), the model has been continuously 
developed and extended for over four decades.

DRAINMOD describes the hydrology of poorly drained and artificially drained shallow 
water table soils. It is widely used in agricultural drainage design, drainage water 
management, wetland hydrology analysis, and water quality research.

---

## Model Capabilities

- Continuous simulation of water table depth, subsurface drainage, surface runoff, 
  and evapotranspiration
- Hourly and daily water balance computations
- Controlled drainage and subirrigation simulation
- Wetland hydrology characterization
- Extended modules for nitrogen cycling (DRAINMOD-N II), soil salinity (DRAINMOD-S), 
  forest hydrology (DRAINMOD-Forest), phosphorus (DMPHOS) and crop growth (DRAINMOD-DSSAT)

## Scale of Application

DRAINMOD has been applied at scales ranging from individual field plots to watersheds 
of several thousand hectares across the United States and internationally.
